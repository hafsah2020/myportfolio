How a Lagos Shoemaker Cut Losses from Material Delays and Wrong Sizes

A data dashboard built from everyday business records — revealing where money was leaking and making it stop.

🧵 Custom Shoemaker
📍 Lagos, Nigeria
The Reality

On the Surface, Business Was Fine

A shoemaker in Lagos runs a small but steady business — taking custom orders weekly.

  • Orders are coming in
  • Customers are paying
  • Production is ongoing

But behind the scenes, money is leaking.

  • Materials (leather, soles) are not always available at the market
  • Urgent market runs delay production and increase costs
  • Customers often request the wrong size
  • Adjustments cost about ₦2,000 per pair and waste materials

At the end of the month, profit is lower than expected — but it's not clear why.

The Problem

Three Issues Draining the Business

1
Unplanned Material Shortages

No system to track which materials run out fastest or when to restock.

2
Frequent Sizing Errors

No record of which sizes or shoe types lead to the most complaints or adjustments.

3
No Visibility into Losses

Costs from delays and rework are not tracked, so decisions are based on guesswork.

The Approach

A Simple Dashboard Built on Real Data

I built a simple dashboard that uses everyday business data to show what's really happening. The shoemaker only needed to track:

  • Sales — shoe type, size requested, final fit, complaints
  • Materials used
  • Market trips and delays

No complex setup. Just structured records.

What the Dashboard Reveals

After Analysing 300 Sales

₦50k
lost per month from delayed or unavailable materials
18%
sizing error rate in women's heels
  • High adjustment frequency on specific sizes (e.g. EU 41–42)
  • Certain materials running out consistently before others

For the first time, the shoemaker could see exactly where the money was going.

View the Dashboard
What Changed

Decisions Based on Evidence

  • Stocked more of high-demand sizes (EU 41)
  • Reduced production of sizes with frequent errors
  • Adjusted buying patterns for materials that run out quickly
  • Introduced a simple size guide for customers
The Result

Real, Measurable Outcomes

  • ↓ 25% reduction in adjustment costs
  • ✓ Fewer urgent market trips
  • ✓ Faster order completion
  • ✓ More "perfect fit" deliveries — no complaints
  • ↑ Improved customer satisfaction and repeat business

This isn't just about the AI automation hype. It's about tracking the right data, making patterns visible, and acting on simple, clear insights.

For small businesses, that's enough to increase profit and reduce stress.
