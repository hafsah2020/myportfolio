Meet the Client

Meet Adaeze: A One-Woman Fashion Studio

Adaeze is a bespoke dressmaker on Lagos Island. Ankara wraps, evening gowns, matching sets — her work speaks for itself. Orders come in via Instagram DMs and WhatsApp. She sews everything herself.

The Struggle

Talented — But Operating Without Direction

  • No SMART goals — she had dreams but no targets with numbers or timelines
  • Fabric waste and over-purchasing — bought what looked good, not what orders actually needed
  • No tracking of peak periods — December chaos surprised her every single year
  • Inconsistent pricing — quoted by instinct, often undercharging complex dress styles

The SMART Framework

Goals Turned Into Measurable Targets

S · SpecificGrow monthly revenue from ₦200k to ₦320k by capping orders at 18/month and prioritising styles priced above ₦18,000.
M · MeasurableTrack weekly: orders received, completed, fabric cost per dress, and net revenue. Review every Sunday evening.
A · AchievableCurrent capacity: 15–20 dresses/month. Target is realistic by replacing 3 low-margin orders with 3 premium ones.
R · RelevantRevenue growth directly funds hiring one apprentice by Month 4, reducing workload and increasing output.
T · Time-bound90 days: Month 1 builds tracking habits. Month 2 applies pricing changes. Month 3 measures full impact.
What Was Done

The Intervention — Business Language Only

  • Mapped her last 6 months of orders, revenue, and fabric spend from WhatsApp records
  • Identified her top 3 most profitable dress styles via contribution margin analysis
  • Classified her order calendar — mapped peak and slow periods across the year
  • Built a simple cost-per-dress calculator to fix her pricing structure
  • Created a 90-day SMART goal framework tailored to her business stage
  • Designed a one-page weekly planning sheet — 15 minutes every Sunday
