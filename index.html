About Me

Energy Sustainability Professional

Growing up in Nigeria, energy poverty was real. People lost opportunities because there was no light, families cancelled plans, and young people jumped from house to house looking for somewhere to charge a phone or finish an assignment. And yet Nigeria sits on some of the largest natural gas reserves in the world, gas that rather than being preserved as an energy asset, is flared into the atmosphere. That gap between what exists beneath the surface and what reaches people above it is what drove me to understand how energy systems work, and how to help fix them.

Watching the industry, I saw companies genuinely trying to navigate the transition, while communities in the Niger Delta bore costs they never agreed to, and households and businesses made energy decisions entirely by guesswork with no data to guide them. The missing ingredient, at every scale, is the same: the right data, at the right time, in a form people can actually use.

I am an energy data scientist with a foundation in petroleum geoscience, built through a BSc in Geophysics from the University of Lagos, an internship at Oando Energy Resource, and a data analyst role at DEER Nigeria. My expertise spans Python, machine learning, geospatial analysis, satellite remote sensing, alongside growing knowledge in ESG frameworks, CCUS, and methane detection. I have also led communities as Campus Lead for GDSC UNILAG, President of the NMGS UNILAG Chapter, and a Millennium Fellow, always with genuine consideration for the people around me.

I am actively seeking graduate study opportunities and early‑career roles across ESG data engineering, energy analytics, CCUS, and energy lifecycle management. Wherever data can make energy more transparent, more accountable, and more accessible – that's where I want to be.
